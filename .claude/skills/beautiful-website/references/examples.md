# Sites I Admire

These are websites I think are beautifully designed. Use them as inspiration and reference points — not to copy, but to understand my taste.

---

## Professional / Corporate / Fintech

### [Stripe](https://stripe.com)
- **What I love:** The gradient mesh backgrounds, the attention to micro-interactions, the way it feels premium without being stuffy
- **Steal this:** Their approach to making technical content feel visually exciting
- **Mood:** Refined, modern, quietly confident

### [Linear](https://linear.app)
- **What I love:** The dark theme done right, the speed and polish, the way every detail feels considered
- **Steal this:** The crisp typography and how they use motion sparingly but effectively
- **Mood:** Fast, precise, developer-luxe

### [Locomotive](https://locomotive.agency)
- **What I love:** Immersive project storytelling where each case study feels like its own world, smooth scroll-driven animations that enhance rather than distract
- **Steal this:** The way they let portfolio pieces breathe with generous whitespace and full-bleed imagery — every project gets cinematic treatment
- **Mood:** Bold, auteur-level confidence, editorial craftsmanship

### [Brex](https://brex.com)
- **What I love:** A sophisticated design system that makes fintech feel approachable, the pricing page is a masterclass in presenting complex tiers simply
- **Steal this:** Their use of subtle gradients and soft shadows to create depth without heaviness, plus the way data visualizations feel like product art
- **Mood:** Polished, trustworthy, grown-up startup energy

### [Wise](https://wise.com)
- **What I love:** Minimalist execution with a bold green brand color that never feels overwhelming, the embedded currency calculator is functional design at its finest
- **Steal this:** How they turn a utility product into something that feels friendly and transparent — the real-time fee comparison is both beautiful and persuasive
- **Mood:** Honest, global, quietly radical

### [Hummingbird](https://hummingbird.co)
- **What I love:** Takes complex compliance and anti-money-laundering data and makes it feel beautiful and navigable, strong information hierarchy
- **Steal this:** The approach to making dense, data-heavy products feel light — clear section breaks, thoughtful use of illustration, and restrained color palette
- **Mood:** Serious but elegant, smart complexity

## Healthcare / Wellness

### [Parsley Health](https://parsleyhealth.com)
- **What I love:** Biophilic design with warm earth tones that feel nurturing, not clinical — it breaks every cold healthcare stereotype
- **Steal this:** The organic shapes and botanical imagery woven into the UI, plus their typography pairing of soft serifs with clean sans-serifs
- **Mood:** Warm, holistic, modern wellness

### [One Medical](https://onemedical.com)
- **What I love:** Clean modern healthcare design that uses green accents to signal wellness without cliché, mobile-first experience is flawless
- **Steal this:** Their approach to appointment booking flow — it feels like booking a restaurant, not a doctor, with clear CTAs and minimal friction
- **Mood:** Accessible, calm, human-first healthcare

## Art / Creative / Culture

### [Gufram](https://gufram.it)
- **What I love:** Bold avant-garde furniture presented with the drama it deserves — the site itself feels like a gallery installation
- **Steal this:** The balance between spectacle and usability — wild product photography that still lets you navigate, shop, and explore without confusion
- **Mood:** Provocative, playful, Italian maximalism

### [Buffalo AKG Art Museum](https://buffaloakg.org)
- **What I love:** Smart collection filtering and exhibition layouts that let the art dominate, the architecture of the site mirrors the museum's physical design
- **Steal this:** Their approach to image-heavy content — lazy-loading done right, responsive image grids that reflow beautifully, and text that knows when to step back
- **Mood:** Institutional but alive, contemporary, respectful of the art

### [Van Gogh Museum](https://vangoghmuseum.nl)
- **What I love:** Vibrant color drawn directly from the artist's palette, the "Vincent for Scale" interactive feature is brilliant UX storytelling
- **Steal this:** How they translate a single artist's visual language into an entire design system — color, texture, and movement all reference the work without being kitschy
- **Mood:** Vibrant, educational, joyful reverence

## Editorial / Media

### [Rolling Stone](https://rollingstone.com)
- **What I love:** Bold typography as brand identity — the headers hit hard, the layout has real editorial energy that makes you want to scroll
- **Steal this:** Their confident use of oversized type and how they mix photography scales (massive hero images alongside tight thumbnail grids) for visual rhythm
- **Mood:** Iconic, loud, cultural authority

### [The New Yorker](https://newyorker.com)
- **What I love:** Print-to-web sophistication done better than almost anyone — the Irvin typeface, the illustration style, the careful reading experience
- **Steal this:** The restraint and consistency — every element serves the reading experience, and the signature illustrations create instant brand recognition across any format
- **Mood:** Literary, refined, quiet intellectual confidence

---

## E-commerce / Consumer

### [Everlane](https://everlane.com)
- **What I love:** Minimalist luxury that puts product first, their "radical transparency" pricing breakdowns are both beautiful and persuasive
- **Steal this:** The micro-interactions on product cards and how photography does 90% of the design work — clean grids, neutral palettes, and letting materials speak
- **Mood:** Honest luxury, California minimalism, intentional

### [Luminaire](https://luminaire.com)
- **What I love:** Serif typography used as a luxury signal, masterful negative space that makes every lighting fixture feel like a museum piece
- **Steal this:** The way they use whitespace as a design element — products float in generous margins, and the typography hierarchy guides you without any visual noise
- **Mood:** Gallery-like, curated, quiet opulence

---

## Experimental / Interactive

### [KPR-verse](https://kprverse.com)
- **What I love:** Immersive Three.js storytelling with manga-influenced layout and parallax that actually serves the narrative rather than showing off
- **Steal this:** The courage to break every conventional web layout rule while still maintaining a clear narrative thread — proof that experimental can be navigable
- **Mood:** Cinematic, manga-meets-web, boundary-pushing
