About the Film

Scattered Denial

The Occupational Dangers of Radiation

Every day across the globe, interventional cardiologists and their teams walk into cardiac catheterization laboratories and expose themselves to ionizing radiation to save lives. They guide stents, valves, pacemakers, and other devices through the vascular system using real-time x-ray imaging—standing just feet from the radiation source, sometimes for hours at a stretch, wearing lead aprons that weigh 30 to 40 pounds.

Scattered Denial: The Occupational Dangers of Radiation is a seven-part documentary series that exposes what decades of this work have done to the people behind the masks. The series documents physicians who developed left-sided brain tumors, skin cancers clustered on the side of their bodies closest to the radiation beam, and career-ending spinal injuries from years of bearing the weight of lead. It features the families of those who didn’t survive.

The title comes from the two forces at the center of the story: scatter—the radiation that bounces off the patient and strikes everyone in the room—and denial—the medical profession’s decades-long refusal to confront what that exposure was doing to its own.

Narrated and executive produced by Dr. David G. Rizik, a veteran interventional cardiologist at HonorHealth in Scottsdale, Arizona, the series was filmed across the country over multiple years. Principal photography revealed that approximately 50% of the interventional cardiologists and cath lab nurses the filmmakers met reported orthopedic injuries, and a staggering number had been diagnosed with cancers that tracked the geometry of their radiation exposure.

The six-part series premiered on April 28, 2024 on ScatteredDenial.org. A one-hour version was reformatted for public television and aired nationally on PBS beginning July 2024, reaching stations including Arizona PBS, WOSU (Ohio), and KET (Kentucky). In 2025, the series was honored with a Rocky Mountain Emmy Award. A seventh episode, What Has Changed?, followed in late 2024, documenting the new alliances, technologies, and legislative efforts that emerged in the wake of the film.

The series is not just a chronicle of harm—it is a call to action. It argues that the technology to virtually eliminate occupational radiation exposure already exists, and that the only barrier is institutional will.

Scattered Denial was made possible in part through the generous support of the HonorHealth Foundation and the Occupational Radiation Safety in Interventional Fluoroscopy (ORSIF) foundation. Their commitment to advancing radiation safety awareness and protecting healthcare workers helped bring this story to a national audience.

The Team

The Filmmakers

DR

Dr. David G. Rizik, MD, MSCAI

Executive Producer & Narrator

Dr. David Rizik is the Chief Scientific Officer and Director of Structural Heart and Coronary Interventions at HonorHealth and founder of the HonorHealth Heart Group in Scottsdale, Arizona. A board-certified interventional cardiologist, he has practiced in Maricopa County for over 33 years, specializing in catheter-based treatment of complex coronary artery disease and advanced valvular heart disease.

Dr. Rizik earned his medical degree from St. Louis University, completed his residency at Barnes-Jewish Hospital/Washington University, and fellowships in cardiovascular disease and interventional cardiology at William Beaumont Hospital in Royal Oak, Michigan. He has published over 100 manuscripts and book chapters and was the principal investigator of the first U.S. drug-eluting stent trial. In 2018, he received the designation of Master Interventionalist from the Society for Cardiovascular Angiography and Interventions (SCAI).

A three-time Emmy Award winner, Dr. Rizik conceived Scattered Denial after witnessing colleagues develop cancers and debilitating orthopedic injuries over the course of his career. In 2023, he led HonorHealth Research Institute to become one of the first providers in the U.S. to install advanced radiation shielding systems in its catheterization laboratories. His companion essay, “Scattered Denial: Reflections on the Occupational Hazards and Health Risks Over a 35-Year Career in Interventional Cardiology,” was published in the Journal of the American College of Cardiology (JACC) in December 2024.
